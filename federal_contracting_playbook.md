# Federal Contracting Playbook

## 1. Initial Market Qualification
Before engaging in the federal marketplace, demand must be verified and structural eligibility established.

### Market Intelligence & Demand Verification
* **Identify Target Demand:** Confirm that federal agencies purchase your specific commercial classification of products or services.
* **Analyze Historical Spending:** Utilize free public data repositories—including `sam.gov`, `FPDS` (Federal Procurement Data System), and `USA Spending`—to quantify annual sales volume within your specific industry.
* **Isolate Target Agencies:** Determine which federal departments, sub-agencies, and specific offices execute the largest volume of relevant purchases.
* **Execute Competitor Benchmarking:** Identify peer companies that have successfully sold to the federal government over the past 5 to 10 years. Analyze their target agencies and map the exact contract mechanisms they utilized to secure awards.

### Corporate Registration & Socioeconomic Set-Asides
* **Mandatory Registration:** Complete formal corporate registration via `sam.gov` to establish legal eligibility to receive federal contract awards and secure a Unique Entity ID (UEI).
* **Leverage Small Business Set-Asides:** Ensure corporate structure satisfies SBA size standards to leverage statutory rules: 23% of all primary federal prime contracting dollars are legally reserved exclusively for registered small businesses.
* **Secure Socioeconomic Certifications:** If eligible, formally certify the business under specific socioeconomic classifications to access narrow-competition set-aside pools:
    * Woman-Owned Small Business (WOSB) / Economically Disadvantaged WOSB (EDWOSB)
    * Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business (SDVOSB) / Veteran-Owned Small Business (VOSB)
    * HUBZone Small Business (Historically Underutilized Business Zones)
    * 8(a) Business Development Program

## 2. Procurement Pathways & Contract Vehicles
The federal government operates via segmented purchasing methods. Corporate positioning must align with an agency’s preferred purchasing mechanism.

### Direct & Simplified Acquisition
* **Government Purchase Cards (GPCs):** Position corporate procurement channels to seamlessly process micro-purchases (typically under $10,000) directly via government credit cards, bypassing prolonged contract cycles.
* **Simplified Acquisition Procedures (SAP):** Target requirements falling between the micro-purchase threshold and the Simplified Acquisition Threshold ($250,000), which are structurally reserved for small businesses and feature streamlined evaluation processes.
* **Sole-Source Contracts:** Target sole-source avenues when providing a unique, non-substitutable commercial offering or when utilizing specific socioeconomic statuses (e.g., 8(a), SDVOSB) that permit non-competitive direct awards under statutory thresholds.

### Structured Pre-Competed Vehicles
* **Indefinite Delivery, Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ):** Compete for positions on massive umbrella vehicles or agency-specific contract platforms that stream procurement tasks over fixed multi-year periods.
* **GSA Schedules / Multiple Award Schedules (MAS):** Establish a long-term, government-wide contract providing federal buyers direct access to commercial supplies and services at pre-negotiated, ceiling-tiered pricing.

## 3. Strategic Capture Planning & Engagement

### Navigating the Procurement Hierarchy
* **Target the Acquisition Office:** Avoid wasting business development capital on standard end-users or personnel who lack statutory purchasing authority. Focus engagement exclusively on the formal Acquisitions Office and designated Contracting Officers (CO / KO).
* **Qualify Internal Knowledge:** When communicating with agency contacts, ask explicit clarifying questions to determine their procurement capability and preference:
    1. *"Which specific acquisitions office handles the purchasing for my industry classification?"*
    2. *"What specific contract vehicles does this agency prefer to use for this type of requirement?"*

### Institutional Asset: The Capability Statement
* **Enforce Hyper-Specificity:** Tailor the opening corporate overview section explicitly to the exact opportunity or agency requirement being pursued. Avoid broad generalist messaging.
* **Remove Direct Contact Friction:** Feature direct contact information prominently at the top or center of the document. Include a specific point of contact's name, direct-dial phone number, and individual email address. Do not use generic 1-800 lines or info@ email addresses.
* **Display Actionable Vehicles:** List all active contract vehicles, GSA schedules, NAICS codes, PSC codes, and your UEI clearly on the face of the document to signal ease of procurement to the Contracting Officer.
* **Substitutional Past Performance:** Detail past federal performance metrics. If federal past performance has not yet been achieved, document verified commercial past performance to instill baseline performance confidence.

## 4. Subcontracting & Teaming Frameworks
When a prime contract vehicle is out of reach or a solicitation is structurally restricted, execute a targeted subcontracting or joint-venture approach.

### Framework 1: Building Verified Past Performance
* Use subcontracting strictly as an entry mechanism to stack verified performance data. Partnering as a subcontractor to established defense primes who possess active contracts allows your firm to execute delivery, log performance data, and position the company for future prime bids.

### Framework 2: Socioeconomic Arbitrage
* When an ideal solicitation is restricted via an exclusive set-aside for which you do not qualify, source a qualified certified small business that possesses the designation. Structure a compliant teaming agreement where your offerings are mutually complementary, allowing you to win a share of the award.

### Framework 3: Vehicle Access via Joint Bidding
* If an agency routes a critical procurement through a closed pre-competed vehicle (e.g., GSA MAS or specific IDIQs) that your firm does not hold, execute a partner analysis. Identify and approach existing contract holders on that specific vehicle, presenting a unified, stronger joint proposal.

## 5. Non-Dilutive Federal R&D: The SBIR Program
The Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) program serves as an accelerated, non-dilutive pathway to secure federal funding and achieve sole-source status.

### Strategic Advantages
* **Velocity:** SBIR vehicles drastically compress standard federal contracting timelines from multi-year wait periods down to a matter of months.
* **Funded Business Development:** The government directly finances your market research, customer discovery, and technical adaptation costs.
* **Intellectual Property Preservation:** The federal government retains no intellectual property rights; firms maintain exclusive commercialization rights within the private sector.
* **Bypassing the RFP Competition Cycle:** Successful graduation from an SBIR phase establishes an enduring statutory right to execute future direct, sole-source commercial sales with any federal agency for that technology, without undergoing full open competitive proposal cycles.

### Phases of Execution
* **Phase I (Feasibility & Customer Discovery):** Awards up to $50,000 to $250,000 to identify your specific internal government customer, map precise mission capability gaps, and execute dedicated federal business development.
* **Phase II (Prototype Development & Modification):** Allocates up to $750,000 to $1.5 million to physically modify, scale, and test your commercial product to meet the precise requirements of the agency user validated in Phase I.
* **Phase III (Commercialization & Direct Procurement):** Transition into direct, long-term procurement and sole-source sales across defense and civilian agencies using standard operational budgets, free from further competition.
